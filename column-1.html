Founder Column 01

Beyond the Data Fortress: Igniting the Era of the Digital Town Square

For decades, we have lived under a digital security model defined by the "Data Fortress." To protect sensitive information, the architects of the modern web built centralized, impenetrable servers. These fortresses were a logical response to the structural limits of legacy systems — a defensive crouch in an era of technological infancy. But today, these walls have become silos. While they keep outsiders out, they also block the rightful owners — the individuals — from seeing how their data is used, leaving us alienated from our own digital essence.

To break this deadlock, I propose a radical shift:

Personal information is never truly protected when it is hidden behind fortress walls; it is only protected when its usage is brought into the "light" for verification.

§ 1The Paradox of Protection: From the Fortress to the Plaza

True data sovereignty cannot be achieved through sealing data away. When data is locked in a centralized fortress, the "gatekeeper" becomes the only entity capable of utilizing it. This creates a fundamental power imbalance where the individual loses all visibility and control.

Newnal's philosophy flips this model. We believe in moving data from the opaque Data Fortress to the Times Square Plaza — a transparent infrastructure built on a public blockchain where every interaction is witnessed and verified. In this digital town square, we protect identifying information through encryption, but we expose the usage trails under the bright lights of a distributed ledger. In a fortress, unauthorized use is hidden; in the plaza, every access is recorded, audited, and instantly detectable. This is the birth of Verifiable Ownership.

§ 2The Newnal Phone: A Vehicle for Digital Liberation

To breathe life into this vision, we have developed the Newnal Phone, powered by AIOS, the world's first AI-native operating system. The Newnal Phone subverts the traditional concept of "logging in." Rather than users entering a service and surrendering their data, service agents must perform a Reverse Login, requesting permission to access the user's decentralized data infrastructure.

When the storage of data shifts from centralized corporate servers to a blockchain cloud under the user's absolute control, the individual truly becomes a Data Sovereign — one who owns their digital identity and directly reclaims the value generated from it.

§ 35,000 Units: The Priming Water for a New Movement

This is not a distant vision or a theoretical exercise. We have already moved from philosophy to action. To ignite this paradigm shift and prove that our AIOS can function perfectly in the real world, we have taken the first definitive step.

The initial 5,000 V1 devices we have produced are not merely hardware; they serve as the priming water — the essential catalyst to realize personal data sovereignty. These 5,000 units are the physical evidence that a world where individuals own and verify their data is functional and ready to begin. This spark will ignite a global transition toward a digital era where technology does not replace human pace but extends and protects it.

ConclusionA New Digital Social Contract

The era of the Data Fortress is ending. We must move beyond the secrecy of the fortress and build the infrastructure of the transparent plaza.

A world where data owners verify every usage path in real-time and reclaim the value of their digital lives is no longer a dream — it is a movement that has already begun. By stepping out of the fortress and into the town square with the Newnal Phone, we finally reclaim our full rights as digital citizens.
