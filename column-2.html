Founder Column 02

Beyond the App Store: The Dawn of the 'Agent Place' as a Catalyst for Innovation

In my previous column, I argued for a move beyond the Data Fortress architecture toward a Digital Town Square where individuals control and verify their own data. The reclamation of data sovereignty is not merely about defense; it must naturally give birth to a new economic ecosystem where liberated data can flow. Just as the App Store was the optimal model for the mobile revolution of the past twenty years, the AI computing era now demands a new arena: the Agent Place.

§ 1The 'App Silo' as a Historical Necessity

Before criticizing the closed nature of today's app ecosystem, we must understand why that structure emerged. Given the security and infrastructure limitations of the past, building independent "fortresses" (silos) for each app was the most rational choice to protect sensitive user data. The current system designed by tech giants is a historical result of trying to balance security and convenience within those structural constraints.

However, over time, these once-safe walls have ironically become hurdles to innovation. Users are exhausted by the cycle of downloading, signing up, and authenticating for every new service. More importantly, the fragmented data trapped within these silos prevents services from understanding a user's comprehensive context, blocking the path toward more advanced, hyper-personalized intelligence.

§ 2Agent Place: A New Horizon for Data Sovereignty

The Agent Place I propose is not a confrontation with the existing system but an evolution of the ecosystem through technological progress. When users open their data in the Digital Town Square — with identifying markers removed — countless agent services can propose the most relevant, value-driven suggestions based on that data.

This is the manifestation of the Reverse Login pursued by Newnal AIOS. Instead of users surrendering their data by entering a service, agents authorized by the user perform a Reverse Login to access the user's decentralized data infrastructure. When My Data — scattered across Google, Amazon, Starbucks, and more — is connected around the user in the Plaza, a true personal agent service that understands you better than you know yourself finally becomes possible.

§ 3A New Opportunity for Startups: Revolution Without Data Barriers

The true value of the Agent Place lies in lowering the threshold for innovation. Until now, it was nearly impossible for new startups to cross the Data Moat held by massive platforms, as they had to secure vast amounts of data before even launching a service. In a Plaza where user-authorized data is available, the narrative changes.

Founders can now utilize AI agent functions and rich personal datasets to realize hyper-personalized services based purely on creative ideas, without the need for immense capital or a massive army of developers. We are moving from an era where the data monopolizer wins to an era where the winner is determined by who interprets data most creatively for the benefit of the user.

The Agent Place will be the fertile ground where new businesses, currently stifled by the stagnant app ecosystem, can finally breathe and grow.

ConclusionA Collaborative Ecosystem for Newnal AIOS

Just as the App Store opened the door to the mobile era, the Agent Place will be the heart of the operating system for the AI computing era: Newnal AIOS. This is not an attack on legacy platforms but a proposal for a new alternative to solve real user pain points and return the benefits of technology to users and innovators alike.

In the Agent Place, where data flows transparently and intelligence competes freely, technology will finally become a tool that infinitely extends human potential. This is the future we seek to build through the new order of Newnal.
